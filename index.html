Professional Background

After completing my studies, I began my professional career as an ESG consultant at KPMG. Our team supported banks in adapting traditional risk models to incorporate emerging climate and environmental risks.
As is often the case in consulting, there was frequently a lot of work to do and not much time. This taught me to stay calm under pressure and to manage expectations carefully — for example, explaining to a bank manager when an important presentation required more time, clarifying the reasons for delays, and ensuring that the client still felt well supported.
During this period I also developed strong practical skills in Excel and PowerPoint — tools that have continued to be valuable since then.

Over time, I felt a growing desire to see the impact of my work more directly. I therefore transitioned into a project development role in the renewable energy sector at Verbund.
Here I worked on large-scale solar projects and particularly valued the variety of tasks involved: coordinating multidisciplinary teams, following regulatory developments, and engaging regularly with stakeholders. While I had already focused on organization and structure in earlier roles, working as a project developer allowed me to improve these skills dramatically.
I was also able to shift my mindset from a slightly perfectionistic approach — trying to finish everything and therefore thinking less about the order of completion — to accepting that there is always an abundance of work, and that the truly important skill is to prioritize effectively.

Beyond my formal professional roles, I have repeatedly taken on project responsibility in my free time. Over the past four years, I founded and grew a beach volleyball community from a small group of around 20 people into a network of more than 1,000 participants. I organized weekly beach volleyball games and social events, managed payments and communication, and eventually developed the initiative into a small side business together with two community members.
The experience has been rewarding in many ways and has led to numerous wonderful friendships. As a valuable side effect, I also gained hands-on experience applying the principles of entrepreneurship and business administration.
