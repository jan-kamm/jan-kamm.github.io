Professional Experience: A Deep-Dive

Fresh out of university, I started my professional journey as an ESG consultant at KPMG Germany. My team supported banks in adapting their traditional risk models to incorporate emerging climate-change and environmental risks. I remember my time at KPMG very fondly and I learned a lot. The learning curve was very steep and over the two years, a built a lot of confidence in interacting with high-ranking clients. Often times, as isn't unusual in consulting, there was a lot of work to do and not much time. This taught me to stay calm under pressure but also to become better at expectation management - telling a bank manager that an important presentation needed more time, explaining what caused the delays and making sure that the manager was still as satisfied as possible. I also worked a lot with Excel and PowerPoint during this time - skills that I am grateful for, as they have often come in handy since.

With time, I felt that I wanted to see the impact of my work more directly, to get a better feeling for what my manpower was actually accomplishing in combating climate-change. For this reason, I deliberately transitioned into a project management and development role in the renewable energy sector at Verbund, Austria's largest electricity producer. There, I worked on large-scale solar projects and particularly enjoyed the large range of tasks that came up each day, like coordinating multidisciplinary teams, staying up to date on the current legislative and technical developments as well as spending a substantial amount of time on the phone and face-to-face with various stakeholders. While I had already put a lot of focus on being well-organized in the years before working at Verbund, I was able to improve this skill dramatically as a project manager. Additionally, I was able to shift my mindset from a slightly perfectionistic approach of trying to always finish all the work towards the acceptance that there is always an abundance of work and the important skill is to prioritize correctly.

Beyond my formal professional roles, I have repeatedly taken on project responsibility in non-corporate settings. Over the past three years, I founded and grew a beach volleyball community from a small group of around 20 people into a network of more than 1,000 participants. I organized recurring large-scale events, managed logistics and communication, and ultimately developed the initiative into a small side business. This experience closely mirrors core project management tasks such as planning, stakeholder engagement, risk handling, and long-term ownership.
